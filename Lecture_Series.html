Lecture Series on the Physical Mathematics of Quantum Field Theory

Due to the global COVID-19 health crisis, we were forced to postpone the summer school on the physical mathematics of quantum field theory, which was previously scheduled to take place during August 10th to 14th. While we are unable to travel, we have decided to organize a series of lecture courses covering topics related to those that would be covered in the summer school. These lectures will be streamed live via Zoom, but also recorded and made available for future viewing. In addition, we will provide lecture notes and example problems, with solutions, so that each lecture series can be taken as a graduate level masterclass on a topic around the physical mathematics of quantum field theory.


First Lecture Series - July 27th to 31st


The first masterclass will be on Supersymmetry and Twisting, given by Chris Elliott (UMass Amherst) and Brian Williams (Northeastern University).
The aim of this lecture series is to explain an approach to supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory and its topological twists through the BV-BRST formalism. Prerequisites: some knowledge of differential geometry, basic Lie theory and homological algebra. Any previous experience with classical and quantum field theory would be helpful, but no specific knowledge is assumed.


There will be four 75 minute lectures:

  • Lecture 1 (July 27th): Introduction to classical field theory in the BV-BRST formalism
  • Lecture 2 (July 28th): Symmetry and supersymmetry in classical field theory
  • Lecture 3 (July 29th): Twisting supersymmetric field theories, with examples
  • Lecture 4 (July 30th): Quantization, OPEs, and factorization algebras.

Times 11:00 to 12:15 EDT, followed by a short break and Q&A.
